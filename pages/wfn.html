Our Journey

Year 1

Western Founders Network was the first club that caught my attention during Western University’s busy clubs’ week. The modern design coupled with exciting business and technology-related events stuck with me. Fast forward past rounds of applications and interviews, I was hired on as a Director of Marketing. In this role, I would help bring video and animation content back to the club’s social media presence.

A collection of all the videos that I've created for the club.

Apart from the media work, I was also able to play a role in organizing some of the club’s flagship events.

The Technology Consulting Case Competition (TCC) is one of North America’s largest student-run case competitions of its kind. With over 300 students from a variety of Universities across Ontario and even the United States, the event was a pleasure to document and operate logistically.

Another event was Future View, one of the largest student-run technology conferences in Canada. Here, we welcomed speakers from companies such as Uber Eats, Amazon, IBM and many more for a day filled with workshops and speakers.

Year 2

My second year saw my promotion to Co-VP of Design. In this role, it was my job to oversee the branding for the club while also hiring and mentoring a team of designers. Although this meant that I would have less time to create content, it was still a very rewarding experience to play a management role within the club.

As a VP, this also meant that I was able to work on creating more value for our team. By organizing internal events and providing separate executive-team resources, the club was able to foster a deeper sense of community. I was also happy to receive feedback at the end of the year from members who felt an improvement over last year in terms of making lasting connections within the club.

Although the year was cut short, I am extremely proud of our team's dedication this year in evolving the brand of the club while also dedicating much of this time to personal growth and development!
